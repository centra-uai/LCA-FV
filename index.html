General findings

The comparative Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) reveals that the HJT 3 system outperforms the 1 MW case in most environmental categories, primarily due to its higher efficiency and bifaciality.

HJT 3 (This study):
– Front-side efficiency: 22.8%
– Bifaciality factor: 92%
– Effective equivalent efficiency: approximately 26.6%

1 MW case (Literature):
– Efficiency: 17%
– Bifaciality not considered

This results in a 56% increase in effective energy conversion for HJT 3, which reduces the material and energy inputs per kWh, driving lower impacts in categories such as climate change, resource depletion, and toxicity.

Exceptions to the general trend

Despite the consistent pattern, some impact categories show notably higher or lower differences than the 56% average reduction. These are analyzed below:

a. Significantly lower reductions or increases

Water consumption (WCP): 12,639% increase
The HJT 3 model includes detailed upstream processes (wafer cleaning and polysilicon purification in China), which are highly water-intensive. The 1 MW case omits these steps and assumes dry robotic cleaning. Additionally, ReCiPe 2016 includes water use from hydropower, which contributes further to this increase.

Ionizing radiation (IRP): 443% increase
This increase is driven by the Chinese electricity mix used in upstream production, which has a higher nuclear content than the European mix assumed in the 1 MW case. This is confirmed in the sensitivity analysis of the HJT 3 study.

b. Greater-than-expected reductions

Mineral resource scarcity (SOP): 84.8% reduction
Reflects reduced demand for silver, copper, and aluminum per kWh due to higher efficiency and newer manufacturing technologies in the HJT 3 system. The inventory also reflects optimized metallization techniques.

These cases highlight the importance of system boundary consistency, geographic accuracy, and inventory completeness in comparative LCA studies.
